# Safe UPI Practices

Follow these practices to keep your UPI transactions secure.

**Never share your UPI PIN.** Your bank, UPI app, or any official support representative will never ask for your PIN. Anyone requesting it is a fraudster.

**Verify before you pay.** Always confirm the payee's name shown on the confirmation screen before authorizing. Scammers create VPAs (virtual payment addresses) with similar-looking names.

**Use collect requests carefully.** You do not need to enter your PIN to receive money. If someone asks you to authorize a collect request to receive money, it is a scam — decline immediately.

**Keep your UPI PIN complex.** Use a 6-digit PIN different from your ATM PIN, birthday, or sequential numbers.

**Enable transaction limits.** Set daily UPI transaction limits in your bank app to cap potential losses.

**Report fraudulent transactions immediately.** If you suspect unauthorized activity, call your bank's 24×7 helpline and raise a complaint within 24 hours. Banks have a higher chargeback success rate for promptly reported fraud.

**Screen-sharing risk.** Never share your screen while doing UPI transactions. Screen-sharing apps can expose your PIN to fraudsters.
