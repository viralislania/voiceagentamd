# Safe UPI Practices

**Never share your UPI PIN.** Your bank and any official support channel will never ask for it. Treat a PIN request as fraud.

**Verify the payee name.** Always confirm the name shown on the confirmation screen before authorising. Fraudsters create VPAs with near-identical names.

**Collect requests do not require your PIN to receive money.** If someone asks you to authorise a collect request "to receive" money, decline immediately — that is a scam.

**Use a strong, unique UPI PIN.** Six digits, different from your ATM PIN, birthday, or sequential patterns.

**Set a daily UPI limit.** Cap potential losses by configuring a per-day transfer limit in app settings.

**Never screen-share during a transaction.** Screen-sharing apps can expose your PIN or OTP to a third party.

**Report fraudulent transactions immediately.** Call the 24×7 bank helpline within 24 hours. Prompt reporting significantly improves chargeback success rates.

**Phishing links.** Never click payment links in SMS or WhatsApp from unknown senders. Open your bank app directly instead.
